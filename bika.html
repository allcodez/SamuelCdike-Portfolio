Bika

A mobility app that allows bicycle owners to bike (and cashout) anywhere close to them delivering food, snacks, groceries fruits or drinks. Cyclist recieve orders from a food ordering service and then delivers them to nearby households in their communities.

Role
Product Manager, UX Designer
Duration
6 Weeks
Tools
Figma, Balsamiq, Notion, Trello
Product
Mobile App

General Overview

Food delivery is a huge challenge for food businesses of food tech startup in Nigeria. It either a challenge of delayed deliveries, missing orders or order mishandling.

A faster and leaner method for these deliveries in communities could help eradicate the challenge these startups or food businesses face. Bika was developed to address this problem in a lucrative way through cycling in communities.

Problem Statement

While ordering food online, you may encounter issues such as delayed deliveries, misplaced orders, and stolen orders. This challenge, in turn, causes the annoyance of ordering food online. When this happens, customers find it difficult to order food, groceries, or fruits online. They may even stop buying food online and devalue the service.

\

Goals & Objectives

  • To design a solution that will eradicate the challenges of food delivery.
  • To design a user-friendly application that encourages cycling.
  • To build a platform where people could lease their bicycles for cash.
  • To invent a go-to market product that would support the climate justice.
  • To build a product that empowers the youthand communities through cycling.

Targeted Audience

Bika target the following: bicycle owners, undergraduates seeking side income, job seekers, youths and food businesses.

Research Method

It is critical in the user-centered design process to place the user at the center of all development decisions. By conducting user interviews, I was be able to learn more about users' needs and desire, as well as their background, which would help me create a positive user experience.

As a result of the first two, I was be able to conduct user research interviews, a market survey, and product requirements research with my team.

Design Process

User Research

The user research focuses on understanding users' cycling and online food ordering behaviours needs, thoughts, frustrations, and motivations. A qualitative research method was used, and 30 people were interviewed. The majority of them were middle-class Abuja resident, with thirty percent studying in Lagos and Enugu state, Nigeria. They ranged in age from 18 to 35. Based on the inteview, each participant described his or her experience with ordering food and cycling.

Empathy Map

After reviewing all of the inteviews (a major component of qualitative research), I proceeded to organize notes - as summaries - into an empathy map, in order to investigate diferent perspective of the interviewees; what they say, do, feel, and think.

Hi-Fi Frames

I moved on to my software tool after several iterations on paper. Before deciding on the best iteration to convert to high fidelity, I first design the interaction in low fidelity to test different ideas and iterations.

Home Screen

Home screen is one of the most important screen on the application which displays the online toggle, side nav bar, user’s account, rating, location, orders locations and account balance.

Request Screen

This screen contains an order info from nearby to either be accepted or declined by the cyclist.

Activity Screen

This screen contains the order history of a user - today, weekly and monthly intervals.

Earning Screen

TThis screen contains the user’s earnings on a daily, weekly and monthly basis. You can also withdraw from here, share your earning url and see more details about your earnings.

Profile Screen

This screen contains everything about the account user - profile picture, user ID, address, Government ID for user verification, account settings and a button to log out.
